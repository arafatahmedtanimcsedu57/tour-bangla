About this Experience

The Sundarbans is the world's largest mangrove forest, straddling Bangladesh and India. I spent three incredible days navigating its labyrinthine waterways, spotting Royal Bengal Tigers from a safe distance, and sleeping on a wooden boat under a sky full of stars. The forest is alive in a way that's hard to describe — every rustle in the undergrowth, every splash in the water keeps you on the edge of your seat. We saw saltwater crocodiles, spotted deer, and a huge variety of birds. The guides from Mongla were exceptional — knowledgeable, cautious, and passionate about conservation.

Travel Tips
1

Book your guide from Mongla forest office — avoid unofficial operators for safety.

2

Carry mosquito repellent and long-sleeved clothes — the insects are fierce after sunset.

3

Bring cash — there are no ATMs once you enter the forest area.

4

Stay silent on the boat — noise scares away wildlife, especially deer and birds.
